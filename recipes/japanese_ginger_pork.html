Japanese Ginger Pork Recipe

This recipe uses thinly sliced pork for a quick Japanese-style sauté. It is excellent with rice and steamed veggies on the side. You can also double the recipe and use whole pork chops or chicken breasts for grilling.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons sake
  • 2 tablespoons mirin
  • 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger root
  • 1 pound thinly sliced pork loin
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil

Steps

  1. Mix together soy sauce, sake, mirin, and ginger in a large bowl until combined.
  2. Add sliced pork; stir to coat. Cover the bowl and marinate for about 1 hour.
  3. Heat oil in a large skillet or wok over high heat.
  4. Sauté pork in hot oil until browned and cooked through; discard marinade. An instant-read thermometer inserted into pork should read at least 145 degrees F (63 degrees C).

Tips

  • Do not sauté the pork on low or medium heat, as the juices will not cook fast enough to get a crispy texture.
